Relevance 8.5/10

Almost all recommendations connect clearly to the taste profile. Yellowstone fits the power-family drama thread (Succession, Peaky Blinders). Mindhunter aligns with Black Bird and True Detective. Patriot suits the spy-drama cluster. Trapped fits the Fortitude/Dublin Murders Nordic-noir strand. The Recruit is a reasonable if somewhat thin match. Reasoning Quality 7.5/10

Most reasons are genuinely specific: Patriot's reason cites bleak wit, morally compromised tradecraft, and absurdist tone linking three distinct input shows; Trapped correctly names Fortitude and Dublin Murders as tonal comparators. The Recruit reason is more generic ('fast-paced CIA thriller'), essentially restating the premise rather than identifying a shared narrative device. The Severance entry provides no meaningful reason at all, dragging the average down.

Diversity 6.5/10

The set is dominated by US productions (Yellowstone, The Recruit, Mindhunter, Patriot, Tulsa King, The Leftovers) with only Trapped offering a non-Anglophone perspective. Era clustering is also noticeable — six of the eight real recommendations are post-2014 and most are post-2017. Genres skew heavily toward crime/thriller/drama with little representation of sci-fi depth, comedy, or historical drama despite the viewer's clear appetite for those. The wasted Severance slot further reduces effective diversity.

Metadata Accuracy 7/10

Most ratings and years are plausible. Patriot's release year of 2015 is slightly off — the show premiered in 2015 as a pilot but the full series launched on Amazon in 2017; minor but worth flagging. Tulsa King's IMDb rating of 8.2 is somewhat generous (actual rating hovers closer to 7.8–8.0). The zeroed-out Severance metadata is a structural anomaly that, while intentional, introduces noise into the dataset. All other figures are within acceptable range.

No Overlap 7/10

Severance explicitly appears in the recommendations, which is a hard rule violation. The engine did flag it internally and zeroed out its metadata, showing awareness, but the title still occupies a recommendation slot. This is a partial violation — the intent to exclude is clear but execution failed, so a score of 10 is not warranted and a score of 0 would be too harsh given the self-identification.

Overall Critique

The recommendation set shows genuine curation effort, with several well-targeted picks (Mindhunter, Patriot, Trapped) that cite specific tonal and narrative links to input shows. However, the inclusion of Severance as a named entry — even with zeroed metadata — is an unacceptable execution failure that wastes a recommendation slot and violates the core no-overlap rule. Diversity is the weakest dimension: the set leans heavily on post-2015 US crime-drama and misses an opportunity to surface non-English-language titles, comedies, or sci-fi that the viewer clearly enjoys. Tightening the genre spread and replacing the Severance slot with a genuinely novel pick would significantly improve the set's overall utility.
